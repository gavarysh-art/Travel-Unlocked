💰 Money-Saving Tricks

The €2 Gondola

Take a traghetto — a public gondola that crosses the Grand Canal. Only €2. Same experience, 40x cheaper.

Vaporetto Pass

Single ride: €9.50. But a 24h pass is only €25. Just 3 rides = pays for itself. Always buy the pass if staying 2+ days.

Gondola Trick

Gondola is €80 per BOAT (up to 6 people), not per person. Split with 5 others = €14 each. Ask your hotel to help form a group.

Free Water

Venice has free drinking water fountains everywhere. The water is excellent. Don't waste €3 on bottles.

Entry Ticket 2026

€5 if bought 4+ days in advance at cda.ve.it (ONLY official site). €10 last-minute. Fine: €50-300. Hotel guests are exempt.

Venice is beautiful but full of traps for tourists. Here's everything you need to know to stay safe:

⚠️ Fake Entry Ticket Websites

The ONLY official site is cda.ve.it. There are many scam sites that look official but charge more or steal your data. Do NOT Google it — go directly to cda.ve.it.

⚠️ Eating Near San Marco

Restaurants around Piazza San Marco charge 2-3x more for worse food. Walk 10 minutes in any direction and prices drop dramatically while quality improves.

⚠️ Unvalidated Vaporetto

Always validate your vaporetto ticket at the yellow machine before boarding. Inspectors check and the fine is steep. With a 24h pass, validate only the first time.

⚠️ Fake Murano Glass

Street vendors sell Chinese-made "Murano" glass for €20-40. Real Murano glass is made on Murano island. Visit a real fornace (furnace) to see authentic glass being made.

⚠️ Gondola Price Confusion

Gondoliers sometimes let tourists believe the €80 price is per person. It's per BOAT (up to 6 people). Clarify before boarding and split with others to save.

✅ Tap Water IS Safe

Venice fountains have excellent drinking water. Many tourists buy €3 bottles because they assume fountains are unsafe. They're not — fill up for free!
